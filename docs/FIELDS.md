## Sender Information

### `from_email`
**Default:** Yes

The email address of the sender, extracted and normalized to lowercase.

**Example values:**
- `john.doe@example.com`
- `newsletter@company.com`

**Use cases:**
- Identifying unique senders
- Filtering/grouping emails by sender
- Building contact lists

---

### `from_name`
**Default:** Yes

The display name of the sender, if provided. This is the name that appears in email clients.

**Example values:**
- `John Doe`
- `Amazon.com`
- `"Marketing Team"`
- (empty if not provided)

**Notes:**
- Some automated emails have no name, just an address
- Display names can be spoofed - don't rely on them for security

---

### `sender_domain`
**Default:** Yes

The domain portion of the sender's email address.

**Example values:**
- `gmail.com`
- `company.com`
- `mail.example.org`

**Use cases:**
- Grouping emails by company/organization
- Identifying newsletter sources
- Detecting potential spam patterns

---

### `reply_to`
**Default:** No

The email address from the Reply-To header. Often different from the From address for newsletters, support systems, and marketing emails.

**Example values:**
- `support@company.com`
- `no-reply@notifications.example.com`

**Use cases:**
- Identifying actual reply destinations
- Detecting "no-reply" senders
- Understanding email routing

---

### `reply_to_domain`
**Default:** Yes

The domain from the Reply-To header.

**Example values:**
- `support.company.com`
- `replies.mailservice.com`

**Use cases:**
- Comparing Reply-To vs From domains (mismatch may indicate newsletters/marketing)

---

## Recipient Information

### `delivered_to`
**Default:** Yes

The email address the message was delivered to, from the Delivered-To header.

**Example values:**
- `you@gmail.com`
- `alias+tag@gmail.com`

**Use cases:**
- Identifying which address received the email (useful if you have multiple)
- Tracking use of email aliases

---

### `to`
**Default:** No

The full To header, which may include multiple recipients.

**Example values:**
- `you@gmail.com`
- `team@company.com, manager@company.com`
- `"John Doe" <john@example.com>`

**Notes:**
- May contain display names with addresses
- Multiple recipients are comma-separated
- May differ from Delivered-To for mailing lists

---

### `cc`
**Default:** No

Carbon copy recipients from the CC header.

**Example values:**
- `colleague@company.com`
- (often empty)

---

### `bcc`
**Default:** No

Blind carbon copy recipients. **Note:** This is rarely populated in received emails since BCC recipients are hidden by design.

---

## Content

### `subject`
**Default:** Yes

The email subject line.

**Example values:**
- `Re: Meeting tomorrow`
- `Your order has shipped!`
- `[GitHub] New issue opened`

**Use cases:**
- Searching/filtering by topic
- Categorizing emails
- Extracting patterns (order IDs, ticket numbers, etc.)

---

### `snippet`
**Default:** Yes

A short preview of the email content, provided by Gmail. This is the same text you see in the inbox preview.

**Example values:**
- `Hi John, Thanks for your email. I wanted to follow up on...`

**Characteristics:**
- Usually 100-200 characters
- Automatically generated by Gmail
- Excludes quoted replies
- Good for quick scanning without body extraction

---

### `body_text`
**Default:** No

The full plain text content of the email, extracted and cleaned.

**Characteristics:**
- HTML is converted to readable text
- URLs are preserved
- Links formatted as `text (url)`
- Configurable max length (`bodyMaxChars`)
- Newlines normalized

**Use cases:**
- Full-text search
- Content analysis
- Natural language processing
- Email archiving

**Performance note:** Extracting body content is slower and creates larger files. Use `snippet` if full text isn't needed.

---

### `body_html`
**Default:** No

The raw HTML content of the email body.

**Use cases:**
- Preserving original formatting
- Template analysis
- Converting to other formats later

**Warning:** HTML can be very large and messy. Use `body_text` unless you specifically need HTML.

---

## Metadata

### `date`
**Default:** No

The Date header from the email, showing when it was sent.

**Example values:**
- `Tue, 15 Jan 2025 09:30:00 -0500`
- `Wed, 29 Jan 2025 14:22:33 +0000`

**Notes:**
- Format varies by sender
- This is the *sent* time, not received time
- Can be spoofed by sender

---

### `message_id`
**Default:** No

The unique Message-ID header, a globally unique identifier for this email.

**Example values:**
- `<CADk+AbCdEfGhIjK@mail.gmail.com>`
- `<1234567890.12345678@company.com>`

**Use cases:**
- Deduplication
- Threading analysis
- Linking to replies (which reference parent Message-IDs)

---

### `thread_id`
**Default:** No

Gmail's internal thread ID, grouping related messages in a conversation.

**Example values:**
- `18d5a2b3c4e5f678`

**Use cases:**
- Analyzing conversation chains
- Grouping related emails
- Understanding reply patterns

**Note:** This is Gmail-specific and won't match Message-ID.

---

### `labels`
**Default:** No

Gmail labels applied to this message, semicolon-separated.

**Example values:**
- `INBOX;CATEGORY_UPDATES`
- `INBOX;STARRED;IMPORTANT`
- `SENT;IMPORTANT`

**Common system labels:**
- `INBOX` - In inbox
- `SENT` - Sent mail
- `TRASH` - In trash
- `SPAM` - Marked as spam
- `DRAFT` - Draft message
- `STARRED` - Starred
- `IMPORTANT` - Marked important
- `CATEGORY_PERSONAL`, `CATEGORY_SOCIAL`, `CATEGORY_PROMOTIONS`, `CATEGORY_UPDATES`, `CATEGORY_FORUMS` - Gmail categories
- `UNREAD` - Not read yet

**Custom labels** appear by their name (e.g., `Work`, `Project-X`).

---

## Attachments

### `has_attachment`
**Default:** Yes

Boolean indicating whether the email has attachments.

**Values:**
- `true`
- `false`

**Use cases:**
- Filtering emails with/without attachments
- Quick attachment detection

---

### `attachment_types`
**Default:** Yes

File extensions of attachments, semicolon-separated.

**Example values:**
- `pdf`
- `pdf;docx;xlsx`
- `jpg;png`
- (empty if no attachments)

**Use cases:**
- Finding emails with specific file types
- Document type analysis

**Note:** If extension can't be determined, MIME type or `unknown` is used.

---

### `attachment_count`
**Default:** No

Number of attachments in the email.

**Example values:**
- `0`
- `1`
- `5`

---

### `has_list_unsubscribe`
**Default:** Yes

Whether the email has a List-Unsubscribe header, which is a strong indicator of newsletters, marketing emails, or automated messages.

**Values:**
- `true`
- `false`

**Use cases:**
- Identifying newsletters
- Filtering promotional vs personal emails
- Compliance tracking for marketing

---

## Field Selection Tips

### For Quick Analysis
```json
"fields": ["from_email", "subject", "date", "snippet"]
```

### For Newsletter Tracking
```json
"fields": ["from_email", "sender_domain", "subject", "has_list_unsubscribe", "date"]
```

### For Attachment Inventory
```json
"fields": ["from_email", "subject", "date", "has_attachment", "attachment_types", "attachment_count"]
```

### For Full Archive
```json
"fields": ["from_email", "from_name", "to", "cc", "subject", "date", "message_id", "thread_id", "labels", "body_text", "has_attachment", "attachment_types"]
```

### For Contact Extraction
```json
"fields": ["from_email", "from_name", "reply_to", "to", "cc"]
```

## Performance Considerations

**Fast fields** (metadata only):
- All fields except `body_text` and `body_html`

**Slower fields** (require content parsing):
- `body_text` - Requires extracting and converting body
- `body_html` - Requires extracting HTML content

**Large output fields:**
- `body_text` - Can be thousands of characters
- `body_html` - Can be very large with formatting
- `to`, `cc` - Can be long for mailing lists

Use `bodyMaxChars` to limit body field sizes.
